A Lecture in 15 Parts

Technology
& the
Post-Pandemic
Economy

How a global shock rewired work, markets, and the way we live online

01 / 15Overview

The thesis

Technology & the Post-Pandemic Economy

  • Accelerated digital transformation across every sector
  • Remote and hybrid work normalized for knowledge workers
  • E-commerce and digital services surged into mainstream use
  • Automation and AI began reshaping the nature of work itself
02 / 15The Shock

2020

−3.1%
Global GDP, 2020

Economic Shock of the Pandemic

  • Global GDP contraction unprecedented since WWII
  • Mass business closures and layoffs across services
  • Disrupted supply and demand simultaneously
  • Emergency government intervention worldwide
03 / 15Policy

Fiscal & monetary

Government Response

01

Stimulus & Relief

Direct payments, business loans, and rescue programs at historic scale.

02

Low Interest Rates

Central banks slashed rates to keep credit flowing and prop up markets.

03

Unemployment Support

Expanded benefits cushioned millions of displaced workers.

04

Public Spending

Health, infrastructure, and digital services received major investment.

04 / 15Labor

Where work went

Labor Market Shifts

  • Sharp rise in remote-work job postings and acceptance
  • Growth in gig and freelance work as a primary income
  • Persistent labor shortages in healthcare, logistics, hospitality
  • Skill demands shifted toward digital, technical, and adaptable roles
05 / 15Digital

Infrastructure of the new economy

Rise of the Digital Economy

01

Online Platforms

Marketplaces, streaming, and SaaS became the default storefront.

02

Digital Payments

Contactless and online payments leapt years ahead in adoption.

03

Cloud Services

Cloud became critical infrastructure for nearly every business.

04

Data-Driven Decisions

Real-time analytics moved from competitive edge to baseline expectation.

06 / 15Main Street

The squeeze

1 in 3
Small businesses at risk of closure (2020)

Small Business Impact

  • High closure rates, especially in dense urban service zones
  • Rapid shift to online operations and delivery
  • Heavier dependence on digital tools and platforms
  • Recovery uneven — by sector, geography, and ownership
07 / 15Concentration

The other side of the K

Big Tech Growth

  • Market dominance expanded across cloud, ads, and devices
  • User dependency deepened — work, school, social, commerce
  • Innovation cycles accelerated, especially in AI and infrastructure
  • Greater regulatory attention — antitrust, privacy, content
08 / 15Commerce

Years pulled forward

+10yr
E-commerce adoption, accelerated

E-commerce Boom

  • Surge in online shopping across every category
  • Same-day and next-day delivery became table stakes
  • Massive expansion of warehousing and last-mile networks
  • Decline and reformatting of physical retail footprints
09 / 15Supply

Globalization, stress-tested

Supply Chain Disruptions

  • Global shortages and shipping delays cascaded across industries
  • Costs of goods rose; inventories whipsawed between scarcity and glut
  • Shift toward local sourcing, near-shoring, and friend-shoring
  • Long-term investment in resilience, redundancy, and visibility
10 / 15Automation

Machines on the line

Automation & AI Expansion

01

Workplace Automation

Warehouses, factories, and offices adopted automation faster than planned.

02

Manual Labor Shifts

Reduced reliance on routine manual tasks; more humans paired with machines.

03

Tech Skill Demand

Data, software, and AI literacy moved up the hiring priority list.

04

Productivity Gains

Output per worker rose where automation and software took root.

11 / 15Learning

Skills as a half-life

Education & Workforce Training

  • Mass shift to online learning, K–12 through higher ed
  • Rapid growth of digital skills bootcamps and credentials
  • Access gaps widened where broadband or devices were missing
  • Lifelong learning emphasized as careers grow more fluid
12 / 15Consumers

A different shopper

Consumer Behavior Changes

  • Strong preference for convenience and frictionless experience
  • Growth in subscriptions, streaming, and on-demand services
  • Increased savings during early lockdowns; later spent down
  • Spending priorities shifted: home, health, hobbies, mobility
13 / 15Recovery

The aftershock

9.1%
US CPI peak, June 2022

Inflation & Economic Recovery

  • Prices rose sharply across food, energy, and housing
  • Supply-demand imbalance lingered well past lockdowns
  • Wage pressure built as labor markets tightened
  • Policy adjusted: rate hikes, tightening, fiscal pullback
14 / 15Equity

The K-shaped recovery

Inequality & the Digital Divide

  • Unequal access to broadband, devices, and digital fluency
  • Income inequality widened along sector and geography lines
  • Education disparities deepened between connected and not
  • Opportunity gaps expanded where remote work was unreachable
15 / 15Outlook

Looking forward

What endures
is how we work,
learn, and trade.

  • Continued tech integration into daily economic life
  • Hybrid work as a long-term equilibrium, not a phase
  • Innovation-driven growth, especially in AI and energy
  • Need for inclusive policies that close, not widen, the divide
