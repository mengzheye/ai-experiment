My Contribution

How I shaped the experience

I designed and shipped a middle school platform experience that balances student engagement with educator control—turning structured curriculum into something students actually want to use. My core contribution was building the system that connects it all: aligning progression logic, content frameworks, and UI patterns into a scalable experience. This enabled personalized learning for students while giving educators meaningful visibility—shifting the platform from a static tool into an engaging, guided learning environment.

Solution

A progressive journey through clusters, pathways, and Hats

The final experience centers on discovering career "Hats" across 16 clusters through engaging, easy-to-digest activities. Learners begin with broad exploration, progress into clusters and related pathways, and then unlock more specific career content based on their activity history.

The product combines gamified moments like "Your Work Personality," "Unlock Hat Packs," and scenario-based activities with instructor-led lessons. This allows educators to run structured classroom workshops while still giving students a personalized sense of discovery.

Building While Learning

The product needed modularity, feedback, guidance, and multiple ways to learn

Multiple rounds of early testing and product discovery helped us identify the "aha" moments that spark curiosity about new careers. The strongest moments came when learners could connect broad cluster exploration to a specific career Hat and see evidence that their actions were shaping a more personal path.

🧱

Learning activities should be modular and digestible, allowing flexible pacing that adapts to different instructional needs (individual coach or a school administrator).

🔄

The feedback system should provide personalized feedback to unblock users to move from Career Cluster learning to a specific Career Hat.

🖥️

The navigation and interface should reinforce the gamified experience, with timely prompts that guide learners seamlessly to their next activity.

🎛️

Interface should have multi modality of learning can lead to deeper understanding and longer-term retention.

Game Mechanism

Reveal content gradually so exploration becomes direction

The experience is designed as a progressive journey where students unlock career content and role-based experiences through exploration and interaction. Instead of exposing everything at once, content is revealed based on engagement and evolving interests.

The core loop turns student actions into momentum: complete one Cluster Quiz to unlock two new Clusters, complete a Pathway Quiz to unlock advanced Tier 2 clusters, and give ratings to unlock more personalized recommendations.

Development

Designing through agile delivery from Sprint 28 to Sprint 40

During development, we worked in agile mode from Sprint 28 through Sprint 40. The team set up CMS structures for cross-product features and launched the main Cluster Exploration experience in the app.

Because the experience was being designed and built in parallel, we used each sprint to validate specific design questions. The biggest iteration areas were dashboard navigation, the Hat page, and the Profile page.

Design Challenge 01

Make the dashboard more goal-directed

Feedback from school purchasers showed that learners and coaches needed clearer structure and more actionable next steps. Entry points to main learning pages were getting lost in the interface, and the greeting widget pulled focus away from key actions.

I reduced the screen space dedicated to the greeting, merged navigation with the primary next action, and introduced background visuals that reinforced the climbing metaphor. The dashboard became more useful as a launch point for students and a clearer instruction surface for coaches.

Design Challenge 02

Reduce scrolling and make Hat content easier to explore

The Hat page needed to make modular assignment faster and more intuitive for coaches while keeping the student experience playful. A long scrolling page buried key information, and early layouts made it hard to scale activity types.

We shifted to a flipped-card interaction that made information more interactive and easier to scan. The back of the "Player Card" introduced stats, ratings, and labels, giving career content a more game-like credibility while preserving space for larger activity moments.

Design Challenge 03

Turn Profile into a living record of progress

Profile needed to become the student's home base: a place where activities, challenges, and reflections turned into visible progress. Early versions felt fun, but scattered sections made it hard for students to understand what information they had earned or why it mattered.

I moved the profile panel into a stronger central position, grouped information more clearly, and highlighted recent badges to make progress feel visible and rewarding. The goal was to help students connect dream interests to actual high school choices, classes, and extracurriculars.

Launch

Middle School product launched with NEISD students

The Middle School product launched in January with North East ISD students. The launch validated the direction of a more modular, classroom-ready, and student-centered career learning experience.

My main takeaway was that career learning becomes more powerful when the system does not simply ask students to complete content, but helps them recognize themselves inside the content. The design work was about creating that bridge: from district requirements to coach facilitation, from clusters to Hats, and from playful exploration to a clearer next step.
